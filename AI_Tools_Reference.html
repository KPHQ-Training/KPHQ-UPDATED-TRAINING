AI Tools Reference

Every AI and automation tool covered in the training — what each one does, when to use it, and what tier it's available on.

AI Assistants
Claude
Anthropic
Free + Paid

The primary AI assistant used throughout this training. Best for long-form writing, structured prompts, document processing, and working with Projects across sessions.

Use for:
  • Drafting emails, SOPs, reports, and client content
  • Processing meeting transcripts into action items
  • Building and running prompts with the TASK framework
  • Working inside the Command Center
ChatGPT
OpenAI
Free + Paid

General-purpose AI assistant. Strong at conversational tasks, quick research, and image generation (with GPT-4o). Useful as a second opinion when Claude's output needs a different angle.

Use for:
  • Quick research and summarisation tasks
  • Image generation with DALL-E
  • Cross-checking or rephrasing AI outputs
Gemini
Google
Free + Paid

Google's AI assistant. Best choice when working inside Google Workspace (Docs, Sheets, Gmail). Native integration makes it fast for summarising emails or pulling data from Sheets.

Use for:
  • Google Docs and Gmail drafts
  • Summarising Google Sheets data
  • Tasks that stay inside the Google ecosystem
Kimi
Moonshot AI
Free + Paid

A capable general-purpose AI assistant known for very large context windows and strong performance on reasoning and agentic tasks. Worth knowing as an alternative option, especially for tasks involving very long documents.

Use for:
  • Working with very long documents or transcripts
  • A second opinion alongside Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini
GLM / Z.ai
Zhipu AI
Free + Paid

Zhipu AI's GLM model family, offered through the Z.ai platform. Another capable general-purpose AI assistant alternative, useful to know exists as the AI landscape keeps adding credible non-US options.

Use for:
  • General AI assistant tasks as an alternative option
Automation Platforms
Make.com
Make (formerly Integromat)
Free Tier: 1,000 ops/mo

Visual drag-and-drop automation builder. More powerful than Zapier for complex multi-step workflows. Supports conditional logic, routers, and AI steps. Covered extensively in Day 5 and Day 9.

Use for:
  • Multi-step automations with branching logic
  • Connecting Google Sheets, GHL, Slack, and AI in one flow
  • Building social content schedulers and research pipelines
Zapier
Zapier
Free Tier: 100 tasks/mo

Simple trigger-action automation. Best for straightforward two-step automations or when a client already uses it. Easier to set up than Make.com but less powerful for complex flows.

Use for:
  • Simple trigger-action automations (form → email, etc.)
  • Connecting apps without complex logic
  • Quick wins when a client is already on Zapier
GoHighLevel (GHL)
HighLevel
Paid

All-in-one CRM and marketing automation platform. The core platform for most KeyPlayers clients. Replaces separate tools for email, SMS, pipelines, calendars, and workflows. Covered in Days 6 and 7.

Use for:
  • CRM management: contacts, pipelines, opportunities
  • Automated follow-up workflows and sequences
  • Conversations inbox: SMS, email, DM in one place
  • Calendar and appointment management
Meeting & Notes Tools
Fathom
Fathom Video
Free Tier Available

AI meeting note-taker. Joins Zoom, Google Meet, or Teams calls and automatically generates a summary, action items, and transcript. Recommended free-tier pick for VAs.

Use for:
  • Capturing internal team and client meetings
  • Generating action items and follow-up tasks automatically
  • Feeding meeting output into the action pipeline
Granola
Granola
Free + Paid

AI note-taker that runs locally on your machine. Good for in-person meetings or when you can't have a bot join a call. Works by enhancing your own notes with AI.

Use for:
  • In-person meetings where a bot can't join
  • Enhancing rough notes after a call
Speech-to-Text / Dictation
Wispr Flow
Wispr AI
Free + Paid

AI voice dictation app. Speak instead of type, and it turns your speech into clean, formatted text in whatever app you're working in. Works on both Mac and Windows.

Use for:
  • Drafting emails and messages by voice
  • Fast note capture without typing
Hex
Kit Langton (open-source)
Free / Open-Source

A free, open-source voice dictation app: press a hotkey, speak, release, and it pastes the transcription wherever your cursor is. Runs and transcribes locally on your device. Mac-only (Apple Silicon) — not an option for Windows-based VAs.

Use for:
  • Mac users wanting a free Wispr Flow alternative
  • Privacy-conscious dictation (processing stays on-device)
Notes & Knowledge Management
Obsidian.md
Obsidian
Free (paid sync/publish add-ons)

A local-first note-taking app. Notes are stored as plain Markdown files on your own device and linked together into a knowledge graph, instead of being locked inside one company's cloud.

Use for:
  • Building a personal or team knowledge base
  • Linking related notes together over time
NotebookLM
Google
Free + Paid

Google's AI research tool. Upload your own documents and sources, and it answers questions, summarises, and generates content (including an audio "podcast" overview) strictly grounded in what you gave it, which reduces the risk of hallucination.

Use for:
  • Researching and querying a specific set of documents
  • Turning client materials into a quick audio overview
Project Management & Communication
ClickUp
ClickUp
Free Tier Available

Project management platform. Used by many KeyPlayers clients to track tasks, projects, and team work. VAs use it to receive assigned tasks, update statuses, and log work. AI features available on paid tiers.

Use for:
  • Receiving and managing task assignments
  • Logging meeting action items
  • Tracking project progress for clients
Slack
Salesforce
Free Tier Available

Team messaging platform. Standard communication tool for VA-to-client and VA-to-team communication. Emoji reactions serve as lightweight workflow signals (✅ = done, 👀 = reviewing).

Use for:
  • Day-to-day communication with client teams
  • Receiving briefs, sharing deliverables
  • Automation notifications (Make.com → Slack alerts)
KeyPlayers Platform
Command Center
KeyPlayers HQ
KeyPlayers Platform

KeyPlayers' internal AI operations platform. A system of 20+ specialized AI agents that VAs direct and manage as AI operators. VAs brief the right agent, review the output, and approve or redirect before delivery.

Use for:
  • Executing client work through specialized AI agents
  • Reviewing and approving AI output before client delivery
  • Operating as an AI operator — directing agents, not just using chat
