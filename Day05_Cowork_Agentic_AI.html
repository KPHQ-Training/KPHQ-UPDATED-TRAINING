Session 1: What is Cowork Mode?

Until now, you have used AI as a Chatbot. You ask a question, it types an answer. You copy the answer and paste it into an email. Cowork mode changes this paradigm entirely.

Standard Claude.ai
You ask, it writes. You do the copying, pasting, and clicking.
Cowork Mode
You assign, it does. It works directly inside your connected apps.

How Cowork Operates

Cowork is a feature within the Claude Desktop App that utilizes MCP (Model Context Protocol) to securely connect to your local files and cloud applications.

ConnectionWhat It DoesExample Prompt
SlackReads channels, sends messages"Summarize the #marketing channel from today."
GmailReads inbox, drafts replies"Draft a reply to the latest email from John."
FilesReads and edits local documents"Organize the files in my Downloads folder."
The Human Margin: Permission Control

Cowork has the ability to read your emails and send Slack messages, but it cannot do so without your permission. The Human Margin is your role as the gatekeeper. When Cowork asks, "Can I send this email to John?", you must verify the tone, check the facts, and click 'Approve'. You are the supervisor; Cowork is the intern.

5-Minute Drill: The Cowork Audit

List three repetitive tasks you do every day that involve copying data from one app (like Slack) and pasting it into another (like Gmail or a Google Doc). Write down how a Cowork MCP connection could eliminate that manual copying.

Session 2: Cowork Power Workflows

Let's look at how Cowork mode transforms standard 30-minute VA tasks into 3-minute operations.

Workflow 1: The Morning Briefing

Manual (20 mins)
Open Slack, scroll through 5 channels. Open Gmail, scan 30 emails. Check Google Calendar. Write notes.
Cowork (2 mins)
"Give me a morning briefing: unread Slack highlights, top 5 priority emails, and today's calendar conflicts."

Workflow 2: Meeting Preparation

Manual (25 mins)
Search for last week's notes. Find the email thread with the client. Draft a new agenda document.
Cowork (3 mins)
"Prep for the 2pm Pacific Realty meeting. Pull context from the last 3 Slack threads and draft a 5-point agenda."
Case Study: The Inbox Zero Hero

A KeyPlayers VA was managing an inbox that received 100+ customer support emails a day. Manually reading and sorting them took 2 hours. By using Cowork, the VA simply prompted: "Read all unread emails. Draft polite replies to any refund requests using the standard policy, and flag any urgent technical issues for my review." The 2-hour task became a 15-minute review and approval process.

The Human Margin: Contextual Blindspots

Cowork can pull the last 3 Slack threads, but it doesn't know that the client called you on your cell phone yesterday to change the project direction. The Human Margin requires you to read the Cowork-generated agenda and manually inject the "offline" context that the AI couldn't possibly know.

Session 3: The Shift to Agentic AI

Cowork is your first introduction to "Agentic AI." Understanding this concept is crucial for your future at KeyPlayers HQ.

The Evolution of AI

  • Level 1: Chatbot. It answers questions. (e.g., ChatGPT web interface)
  • Level 2: Assistant. It completes specific, single-step tasks. (e.g., "Write an email")
  • Level 3: Agent. It orchestrates multi-step workflows across systems. (e.g., "Find the lead, draft the email, check my calendar, schedule a reminder.")

Voice-to-Action with Wispr Flow

Wispr Flow is a dictation tool that types what you speak. When combined with an Agentic AI like Cowork, you achieve Voice-to-Action.

"Hey Claude, check my inbox for anything from Sarah, draft a polite decline to her meeting request, and send me a Slack message when it's staged."

It will take Agentic AI to the next level by deploying specialized agents (Sales Agent, Social Agent) directly from KeyBrain. Your job will transition from "doing the tasks" to "managing the agents doing the tasks." You will be the human margin overseeing the entire operation.

Session 4: Build an Agentic Prompt

To use Agentic AI effectively, your prompts must evolve. You are no longer asking for text; you are programming a workflow.

The Agentic Prompt Structure

  1. Trigger: What initiates the workflow?
  2. Sources: Where should the AI look for data?
  3. Logic: How should it prioritize or filter that data?
  4. Action: What should it actually do with the result?
Run my End-of-Day Wrap Up: 1. Sources: Check Gmail for unread emails and Slack for mentions of my name. 2. Logic: Ignore newsletters. Flag anything from the domain "@pacificrealty.com" as High Priority. 3. Action 1: Draft short, polite "I will look into this tomorrow" replies for the High Priority items. 4. Action 2: Create a bulleted summary of everything else and save it to my Desktop as "EOD_Summary.txt".
The Human Margin: Edge Case Handling

Agentic prompts work 90% of the time. The Human Margin is required for the 10% edge cases. If an email comes in from a new investor who doesn't use the "@pacificrealty.com" domain, the AI will miss it. You must always do a final manual scan to catch what the rigid logic of the AI missed.
