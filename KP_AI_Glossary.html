AI Glossary

Plain-language definitions for every AI term in the program.

60+ terms · Weeks 1 & 2 reference · No jargon, just clarity.

If a word in the course confused you, it is in here. Every definition is written the way you would explain it to a friend, with no tech background required. Where a metaphor helps, we used one. You do not need to memorize these. Keep this page open and refer to it whenever you need it.

A
Agent / AI Agent Advanced
An AI that does not just answer questions. It takes actions on your behalf. You give it a goal, and it figures out the steps, uses tools, and completes the task. Example: an AI agent could receive a client email, look up their CRM record, draft a reply, and send it, all without you clicking anything. This is where AI is heading.
hiring a contractor who figures out the project plan themselves, instead of someone who needs you to give them every instruction one at a time.
Agentic AI Advanced
A way of using AI where it works through multi-step tasks on its own, rather than waiting for you to guide each step. Think of it as the difference between an AI you prompt once and one you give a whole job to. Most VA tasks today are not yet fully agentic, but the tools are moving in that direction.
AI (Artificial Intelligence) AI
Technology that lets a computer do things that normally require human thinking, such as reading, writing, answering questions, and making decisions. The AI tools you will use as a KP VA are built on this.
API (Application Programming Interface) Technical
A connection between two apps that lets them share information automatically, with no human doing it manually. When a form submission in your CRM automatically triggers an email, an API is doing that work in the background. You will not build APIs yourself, but understanding the term helps when clients or tools mention it.
a waiter in a restaurant. You tell the waiter your order, they take it to the kitchen, and the kitchen sends back the food. You never deal with the kitchen directly.
Artifacts Claude
In Claude, an artifact is a piece of finished output (a document, code, a table, or a chart) that Claude creates as a self-contained file rather than just text in the chat window. You can copy, download, and use it directly. When you ask Claude to "write a report" or "make a table," the output often arrives as an artifact.
Automation Automation
When a system completes a task on its own, based on a trigger you set up, without you doing it manually each time. Example: when a new lead fills out a form, an automation can send a welcome email, add them to the CRM, and notify you, with no human input. In GHL, automations are built using Workflows.
setting up a coffee maker on a timer so it brews before you wake up. You did the setup once, and it runs every day without you.
Avatar (AI) AI
A digital character that speaks on screen in AI-generated videos, used in tools like HeyGen. You write the script, choose an avatar, and it delivers the message visually, with no camera or recording required.

B
Benchmarking AI
A way of testing and comparing AI models to see which performs best on specific tasks. Think of it as a standardized test for AI. Benchmarks measure things like accuracy, reasoning, and speed. When you hear that a new model "tops the benchmarks," it means it scored higher on those standard tests. Useful to know when choosing which model to use for a task.
Bot Automation
A program that runs automated tasks, usually repetitive ones. A chatbot responds to messages. A workflow bot might send emails or update records. Bots follow fixed rules, so they are not the same as AI agents, which can reason and adapt.
↑ Back to top
C
Chain of Thought Prompting
A prompting technique where you ask the AI to think through a problem step by step before giving its final answer. This leads to more accurate results on complex tasks. Example: instead of asking "What is the best follow-up sequence for a lead?", you ask it to walk you through the reasoning, step by step. The thinking process improves the answer.
Chatbot AI
A program that responds to typed or spoken messages. AI chatbots like Claude can hold real conversations, answer questions, write content, and assist with tasks. They are different from older rule-based chatbots that could only follow scripts.
Claude Claude
Anthropic's AI assistant. Claude is the primary AI tool used in the KP stack for writing, summarizing, analyzing, and task support. You will interact with Claude through claude.ai.
Claude Pro Claude
Anthropic's paid plan for Claude. This is the standard plan for KP VAs. It gives higher usage limits than the free plan and access to all of Claude's core features, enough for full-time professional work.
CLI (Command Line Interface) Technical
A way of controlling a computer by typing text commands instead of clicking buttons. Instead of opening an app, you type a command in a terminal window and press Enter. Tools like Claude Code run through a CLI. As a VA you will rarely use this directly, but you may see it when setting up tools or following technical instructions.
ordering at a restaurant by writing a note instead of pointing at the menu. It is more direct, but it requires knowing exactly what to write.
Closed Source Privacy
Software where the code is private. You can use the product, but you cannot see or modify how it was built. Most commercial AI tools are closed source. Knowing this matters when clients ask about data privacy or tool reliability.
Context Engineering Prompting
The skill of designing and managing the information you give an AI so it does its best work. It goes beyond writing a good prompt: it includes deciding what background to include, what to leave out, how to structure the conversation, and whether to use Projects or system prompts. Think of it as setting up the AI's workspace before asking it to do a task.
briefing a new employee before their first day. The better the brief, the better the work.
Context Window Claude
The amount of information Claude can hold in memory during a single conversation. If a conversation gets very long, Claude may not fully recall what was said at the very beginning, because it has filled its context window. Starting fresh or using Projects keeps Claude focused and accurate. Different models have different context window sizes, and bigger is generally better for long, complex tasks.
a whiteboard with limited space. Once it fills up, you have to erase the old notes to write new ones.
Crawler / Web Crawler Technical
An automated program that visits websites and reads their content systematically. Search engines like Google use crawlers to index the internet. AI companies have also used crawlers to gather text from public websites when training their models. If a client asks whether their website content might be in an AI's training data, the honest answer is that it was likely crawled at some point.
a researcher who reads every book in a library, page by page, to learn everything in it, except this researcher moves at computer speed and covers the entire internet.
CRM (Customer Relationship Management) GHL
Software for tracking client and lead information: contacts, conversations, deals, and tasks. The KP stack uses GoHighLevel (GHL) as its primary CRM, with HubSpot or Zoho depending on the client.

D
DALL-E AI Tool
OpenAI's original image generation tool. You describe an image in words and it creates one. Inside ChatGPT, image generation is now built directly into OpenAI's main models, which replaced the standalone DALL-E, and it is available to both free and paid users, with higher limits on paid plans. You will still hear the DALL-E name used as shorthand for AI image creation.

E
E2EE (End-to-End Encryption) Security
A security method where only the sender and the receiver can read a message, not even the company running the service. The data is scrambled (encrypted) before it leaves your device and only unscrambled when it arrives at the other end. WhatsApp uses E2EE. Most AI tools, including Claude, do not, which means the provider can technically access your conversations. Treat anything you type into an AI tool as visible to that company, and avoid pasting sensitive client data unless the client has approved it.
putting a letter in a locked box where only the recipient has the key. Even the delivery person cannot read it.
↑ Back to top
F
Few-Shot Prompting Prompting
A prompting technique where you include a few examples in your prompt to show the AI the format or style you want. Example: "Here are two examples of how I write follow-up emails: [example 1] [example 2]. Now write one for this situation: [situation]." Giving examples produces more consistent results than describing the format in words.
Fine-tuning Advanced
The process of taking a base AI model and retraining it on a specific dataset so it specializes for a particular task, style, or domain. Unlike prompting — which shapes what the model does in a conversation — fine-tuning changes what the model fundamentally knows and how it responds. It requires technical access and significant data. As a VA, awareness only: if a client asks to "train AI on our brand voice," this is what they mean, and it is an engineering project, not a VA task.
the difference between coaching an employee with instructions every day (prompting) versus putting them through a full specialized training program that changes how they think (fine-tuning).
↑ Back to top
G
Generative AI AI
AI that creates new content (text, images, video, or code) based on a prompt. Claude, ChatGPT, and HeyGen are all generative AI tools. The name comes from the fact that the AI generates something new, rather than just looking it up.
GHL (GoHighLevel) GHL
The primary CRM and automation platform in the KP stack. GHL manages leads, pipelines, appointments, SMS and email campaigns, sub-accounts, and automations for clients.
↑ Back to top
H
Hallucination AI
When an AI confidently states something that is incorrect. It is not lying; it genuinely does not know it is wrong. This is why you always verify key facts, dates, and figures that Claude produces before sending them to a client. Never skip this step.
a confident coworker who gives you the wrong directions but sounds completely certain. You still need to double-check before you drive anywhere.
HeyGen AI Tool
An AI video tool that uses digital avatars to deliver spoken scripts. You write the script, choose an avatar, and HeyGen creates the video, with no camera or recording required.
↑ Back to top
I
Image Generation AI
Using AI to create an original image from a text description (called a prompt). Tools like ChatGPT's built-in image generation and Midjourney do this. The result is a completely new image, not a stock photo.
Image-to-Video AI
A feature in tools like Kling, Runway, and Google Veo that takes a still image and adds motion, such as a slow zoom, a camera pan, or animation. Used to create video content from AI-generated images.
Inference Technical
The act of running a trained AI model to generate a response. When you send a message to Claude and it replies, that is inference happening. The model was trained once on massive data; inference is what happens every single time you use it. This is where the compute cost comes from — each reply is processed on powerful servers in real time, not on your device. Faster, cheaper models are optimized for faster inference. Premium models run heavier inference for more complex reasoning.
a musician performing live. The years of practice were the training. Every concert is inference — applying what was learned, on demand.
Iteration Prompting
Going through multiple rounds of feedback and revision with an AI tool. Example: Claude gives you a draft, you ask it to shorten it, then you ask it to make the tone warmer. Each change is an iteration. Good prompts often take two or three iterations to get right. Knowing how to refine is as important as knowing how to prompt.

J
JSON (JavaScript Object Notation) Format
A common file format used to send structured data between apps and APIs. It looks like a list of labels and values inside curly brackets. You will occasionally see JSON when working with integrations or automation tools. You do not need to write it, but recognizing it helps when reading tool documentation or automation logs.
{"client": "Acme Corp", "status": "active", "leads": 24}
↑ Back to top
L
Live Artifact Claude
A special type of artifact in Claude's Cowork mode that stays updated over time. Instead of a static document, a live artifact connects to data sources and refreshes automatically, like a dashboard that shows current project status without you rebuilding it each time. Live artifacts are more advanced than regular artifacts and are useful for recurring reports or trackers.
a digital scoreboard that updates in real time, instead of a printed leaderboard that is out of date the moment you print it.
LLM (Large Language Model) AI
The technology behind AI tools like Claude and ChatGPT. LLMs are trained on enormous amounts of text and learn to predict and generate language that sounds natural and accurate. When you type a prompt, the LLM generates a response based on patterns it has learned. Every time you use Claude, you are using an LLM.
someone who has read millions of books and can write in any style, on any topic, but still needs to be given specific direction to do useful work.
↑ Back to top
M
Markdown Format
A simple way of formatting plain text using symbols. Instead of clicking bold or heading buttons like in Word, you type symbols: **bold** becomes bold, # Heading becomes a heading, - item becomes a bullet point. Claude often writes responses in Markdown. Most AI platforms, Notion, and GitHub display it automatically. Understanding Markdown helps you format documents and prompts more precisely.
MCP (Model Context Protocol) Technical
A standard that allows AI tools to connect with external software (such as your CRM, calendar, email, or Slack) and take actions inside those tools directly, without you switching tabs. Introduced by Anthropic, MCP is becoming the common way for AI to do tasks across platforms automatically.
a universal remote that controls every app on your computer. Instead of switching between tools, one AI handles them all.
Metadata Technical
Data that describes other data: information about a file or record, not the content itself. Example: a photo's metadata includes when it was taken, the device used, and the location, but not what the photo shows. In a CRM, metadata about a client record might include who created it, when it was last updated, and its status. AI tools also use metadata to organize and retrieve information efficiently.
the label on a filing folder. It tells you what is inside without you needing to read every page.
Meta Prompting Prompting
Using an AI to help you write better prompts for the AI. Instead of writing a prompt yourself and hoping it works, you ask Claude: "Help me write a prompt that will get the best possible result for [task]." Meta prompting is also used to build prompt templates, by asking Claude to design a reusable prompt structure for recurring tasks.
asking a chef how to write the recipe before you start cooking. You get the instructions right before you execute.
Model AI
The specific version of an AI you are using. Claude Sonnet and Claude Opus are different models with different strengths. Sonnet is fast and good for most tasks. Opus is more thorough for complex work. Haiku is the fastest and lightest. Choosing the right model for the task saves time and usage.
Multimodal AI
An AI that can handle more than one type of input, such as text, images, audio, or video. Claude is multimodal: you can paste an image and ask Claude to describe or analyze it, not just type questions. A multimodal model is more versatile than one that only works with text.
↑ Back to top
O
Open Source Privacy
Software where the code is made publicly available. Anyone can read it, study it, modify it, or build their own version. Open source tools are often free and community-supported. Being open source does not automatically mean the AI is better or safer; it just means it is more transparent about how it works.
↑ Back to top
P
Pipeline GHL
In a CRM, a pipeline is a visual board showing where each lead or client is in the sales or service process, for example: New Lead, Contacted, Proposal Sent, Closed. Reporting on pipelines is a common VA task in GHL.
Projects (Claude) Claude
A feature inside Claude that lets you create separate workspaces, one per client. Each Project stores the client's name, tone, preferences, and background context so Claude always knows who it is helping. Projects prevent cross-client confusion and remove the need to re-explain context in every new conversation.
a dedicated folder for each client on your desk. Everything you need is already in there when you sit down to work.
Prompt Prompting
The instruction or question you type to an AI tool. A good prompt is specific, gives context, and tells the AI what format you want. Better prompts produce better results. Prompting is a skill, and it improves with practice.
Prompt Engineering Prompting
The practice of designing and refining prompts to get the best possible output from an AI. It involves structuring your request, providing context, specifying format, giving examples, and knowing when to break a big task into smaller steps. Prompt engineering is one of the most valuable skills for a VA working with AI tools.
Prompt Library Claude
A personal collection of prompts that have worked well for recurring tasks. Building a Prompt Library saves you from rewriting instructions every time and helps maintain quality and consistency across clients. You will build and maintain one as part of this course.
↑ Back to top
R
RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) Advanced
A technique where an AI retrieves relevant information from a specific source (such as your company documents, client files, or a database) before generating a response. Instead of relying only on what it learned during training, it looks things up in real time. This makes answers more accurate and grounded in current, specific information.
an open-book exam. The AI can look up the right information before answering, rather than relying only on what it memorized.
Remotion AI Tool
A developer tool that creates animated videos using code. Claude writes the code, and Remotion renders it into an MP4 file. Used for text-based motion graphic videos. It is the most technical tool in the KP stack.
Render AI Tool
The process of a computer converting code or instructions into a finished video file. When you render a video in Remotion or HeyGen, the tool is processing all the frames and producing the final MP4.
↑ Back to top
S
Script (Video) AI Tool
The spoken words for a video, written before recording or generation. Claude writes video scripts optimized for AI voices: short sentences, clear language, and natural phrasing.
Skills (Claude / Cowork) Claude
Reusable instruction packages that give Claude specialized capabilities for specific tasks. A Skill is like a playbook: it tells Claude how to approach a particular type of job, such as writing a report, managing a social post, or evaluating an SOP. Skills can be installed in Cowork and used on demand. They save time by removing the need to re-explain complex workflows from scratch each time.
Sub-Account (GHL) GHL
In GoHighLevel, each client you manage has their own separate sub-account. Switching between sub-accounts is how you keep different clients' data separate. Always confirm you are in the right sub-account before working.
System Prompt Claude
A set of instructions given to an AI before the conversation begins. It tells the AI who it is, what rules to follow, what tone to use, and what context to keep in mind. Claude Projects use a system prompt in the background: you do not see it in the chat, but it shapes every response. Writing a good system prompt is a core part of context engineering.
an employee handbook given to a new hire before their first shift. It sets expectations before any work starts.
↑ Back to top
T
Temperature Technical
A setting that controls how creative or predictable an AI's responses are. Low temperature = more focused, consistent, factual answers. High temperature = more varied, creative, and sometimes surprising responses. Most AI chat tools manage this automatically, but if you ever configure an AI via API or automation, temperature is one of the key settings to know.
the difference between a strict recipe (low temperature) and a chef freestyling in the kitchen (high temperature). Both can work depending on what you need.
Token AI
The unit that AI models use to process text. Roughly, one token equals about four characters, or three-quarters of a word. AI plans have usage limits measured in tokens. Long conversations, large documents, and frequent use all consume more tokens. This is why Claude Pro has usage limits, and why keeping context lean helps you get more done per session.
taxi fare on a meter. The longer the trip (the conversation), the more it costs. Short, focused rides are more efficient.
Training Data Advanced
The text, images, and other content that an AI model was exposed to during the learning phase, before it was released. Claude was trained on a large portion of publicly available internet text, books, and other sources. After training, the model is fixed: it does not learn from your conversations in real time. Note: some providers, including Anthropic, may use saved consumer conversations to train future models unless you opt out in settings, so check the data settings on any account that handles client information.
Trigger Automation
In automation, a trigger is the event that starts an automated workflow. Example: "when a new lead fills out a form" is a trigger. Everything after that (the email, the CRM update, the notification) is what the automation does in response.
↑ Back to top
W
Webhook Automation
A way for one app to automatically notify another app when something happens, without either app needing to constantly check in. Example: when a form is submitted in your CRM, a webhook can instantly tell your email tool to send a message. Webhooks are the connective tissue of automation, making integrations fast and real-time.
a doorbell. The moment someone is at the door, you get notified instantly, instead of having to check the door every few minutes yourself.
Workflow GHL
A sequence of steps that gets a task done from start to finish. In GHL, a Workflow is the automation builder where you set triggers, conditions, and actions. In the broader VA context, a workflow is the combination of tools and prompts you use to complete a specific type of task, like producing a client report or posting social media content.
↑ Back to top
Z
Zero-Shot Prompting Prompting
Asking an AI to complete a task without giving it any examples, just the instruction. Most basic prompts are zero-shot. Example: "Write a follow-up email for a lead who went quiet." This works well for simple tasks. For more specific or formatted outputs, few-shot prompting (giving examples) tends to produce better results.
