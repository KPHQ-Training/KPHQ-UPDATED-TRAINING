What Is GoHighLevel?

Before you click a single button, you need the mental model. GoHighLevel isn't one tool -- it's a dozen tools fused into one platform. Understanding what it replaces is the fastest way to understand what it does.

The one-line definition

GoHighLevel (GHL) is an all-in-one CRM and marketing platform that lets a business capture leads, nurture them, close deals, and keep customers -- all in one place.

Most businesses used to stitch together 8--10 separate tools: one for email, one for texting, one for the calendar, one for the sales pipeline, one for landing pages, one for reviews. Each cost money, none of them talked to each other, and the VA had to log into all of them. GoHighLevel replaces that entire stack.

Day 6 · Session 2

Agency vs. Sub-Account -- The Most Important Concept

This trips up almost every new VA, so get it clear now. GoHighLevel has two levels:

A
The Agency Account

The top level. This is owned by the agency (often your client, or KeyPlayers itself). From here you can see and manage every business that uses the platform. Think of it as the building manager's master key. You usually won't live here day-to-day -- but you'll launch from it to jump into individual accounts.

S
The Sub-Account (Location)

Each individual business gets its own sub-account (also called a "location"). One sub-account = one client's business. Their contacts, pipelines, calendars, and messages live here, completely separate from every other business. 99% of your daily work happens inside a sub-account. When a client says "go into my GHL," they mean their sub-account.

The golden rule of multi-client work

Always confirm which sub-account you're inside before you do anything. Sending a campaign or moving a deal in the wrong client's account is the kind of mistake that's hard to undo. The account name is always shown at the top -- check it every time you switch.

The Standard

Right account name confirmed at the top = safe to proceed. If you're not sure, stop and check before touching anything.

GHL's Five Core Areas -- Your Mental Map

Everything inside GHL sits in one of five areas. Internalise this map and you'll always know where to go, even when a client asks for something you haven't done before.

1
Capture

Forms, surveys, funnels, chat widgets. These are the entry points where leads first come in. When a VA adds a form to a website or shares a booking link, they're working in Capture.

2
Nurture

Conversations, automations, email/SMS campaigns. This is where leads get followed up with, warmed up, and kept engaged. Most daily VA messaging work happens here.

3
Close

Pipelines, opportunities, calendars, proposals. Where deals move through stages and appointments get booked. Keeping this layer accurate is one of the highest-value VA tasks.

4
Retain

Reviews, reputation management, memberships. After the sale -- keeping customers happy and generating referrals. Often overlooked, but critical for long-term client success.

5
Report

Dashboards, analytics, attribution. How the owner knows what's working. A VA who pulls a clean weekly report gives the owner visibility without them having to dig for it.

Mental model for daily work

Before you open GHL, ask: am I capturing, nurturing, closing, retaining, or reporting? The answer tells you which module to open. This is how experienced VAs move fast.

Day 6 · Session 4

CRM & Contacts

Contacts are the heart of GoHighLevel. Every lead, customer, and conversation connects back to a contact record. Master this section and the rest of the platform makes sense.

The Contact Record

Click any contact and you open their record -- a complete profile of everything the business knows about that person. It contains:

  • Core details: name, email, phone, address, source (how they came in)
  • Tags: labels you apply to organise and segment (more below)
  • Custom fields: business-specific data -- property type, budget, service requested, etc.
  • Conversation history: every text, email, and call with this person
  • Appointments: past and upcoming bookings
  • Opportunities: which deals/pipelines they're part of
  • Activity & notes: a timeline of everything that's happened

Tags -- Your Organisation Superpower

What a tag is

A tag is a simple label you stick on a contact -- like new-lead hot booked-call past-client. Tags are how you group people so you can message, automate, and report on them. A contact can have many tags.

Tags are the connective tissue of GHL. Almost every automation you'll build on Day 7 starts with "when this tag is added...". Good tagging discipline is what separates an organised account from chaos.

Messy tagging
Random tags like "John", "interested?", "call him", "MAYBE". No system. Nobody else can tell what they mean. Automations break.
Clean tagging
A consistent system: lifecycle tags (lead / opportunity / customer), source tags (fb-ad / referral), and status tags (nurturing / booked / closed). Predictable and automatable.
Day 6 · Session 4

Custom Fields -- Storing What GHL Doesn't Know By Default

Every business collects different information about their leads. A real estate client tracks budget and property type. A med spa tracks treatment interests. Custom fields are where that business-specific data lives inside each contact record.

What a custom field is

Custom fields are like extra columns in a spreadsheet that the business has added for their specific needs -- things like "Budget Range," "Service Requested," "Referral Source," or "Preferred Contact Time." As a VA, you fill these in as you learn more about each lead.

Custom Fields in Practice

Business TypeExample Custom FieldsWhy It Matters
Real EstateProperty type, budget, pre-approved?, beds neededLets agents send targeted listings and prioritise hot buyers
Coaching / ConsultingGoal, program interest, where they heard of usHelps tailor the sales conversation and onboarding
Home ServicesService requested, property size, best call timeRoutes the right technician and cuts back-and-forth
Med Spa / BeautyTreatment interest, skin type, last visit dateEnables personalised follow-ups and package matching
Don't guess at custom field values

If you're unsure what goes in a field, leave it blank and ask the client. Filling in wrong data (wrong budget range, wrong service type) pollutes the record and can fire the wrong automations at the wrong people. Blank is always better than wrong.

Day 6 · Session 4

Smart Lists -- Saved Filtered Views

A Smart List is a saved filter over your contacts. Instead of scrolling thousands of records, you build a view like "all contacts tagged hot-lead who haven't booked a call." Save it, and it updates automatically as contacts match or stop matching.

Common Smart Lists a VA maintains:

  • New leads from the last 7 days (to follow up)
  • Leads with no appointment booked (to chase)
  • No-shows (to re-engage)
  • Customers (for retention campaigns)

Adding & Importing Contacts

1
Add one contact manually

Contacts → Add Contact. Fill in name, email, phone. Apply the correct source tag immediately.

2
Import a list (CSV)

Contacts → Import. Upload a spreadsheet, then map each column to a GHL field. Always apply a tag to the whole batch on import (e.g. webinar-jan) so you can find them later.

3
Check for duplicates

GHL merges by email/phone, but always sanity-check after a big import. Duplicate contacts cause double-messaging -- a fast way to annoy a client's leads.

High-stakes warning

Contact data is real people's personal information, and a client's most valuable asset. Never export, share, or delete contacts without explicit permission. Never test automations on real leads. When practising, create a fake contact with your own details. Treat the database like it's irreplaceable -- because to the client, it is.

Pipelines & Opportunities

A pipeline is the visual map of how a business turns a lead into a paying customer. Keeping it accurate is one of the highest-value things a VA does -- it's how the owner knows where the money is.

The vocabulary

Pipeline = the whole journey, broken into stages (like a Kanban board).

Stage = one column/step in that journey (e.g. New Lead → Contacted → Booked → Won).

Opportunity = one deal for one contact, sitting in one stage, usually with a dollar value attached.

Day 6 · Session 5

Creating an Opportunity -- Step by Step

When a lead is ready to be tracked as a potential sale, you create an opportunity for them. Here's exactly how to do it:

1
Open the contact record

Contacts → find the contact → click their name.

2
Go to the Opportunities tab

Inside the contact record, click Opportunities (usually in the right panel or sub-menu).

3
Click "Add Opportunity"

A panel opens. Fill in: Pipeline (which pipeline this belongs to), Stage (usually "New Lead"), and Opportunity Name (e.g. "Sarah M. -- Kitchen Reno").

4
Add a dollar value

Enter the estimated deal value. Even a rough number helps -- it feeds the pipeline total the owner watches.

5
Save and move the deal forward

The opportunity appears on the pipeline board. Drag it to the correct stage as the relationship progresses.

Incomplete opportunity
No value entered. Named "Lead." No stage set. Pipeline total is meaningless. Owner can't make decisions from this.
Complete opportunity
Named clearly (contact + job type). Value entered. Correct starting stage. Owner has real, actionable pipeline intel.

The VA's Pipeline Checklist

Keep every pipeline healthy
  • Every new lead lands in the first stage with a source tag
  • No deal sits in a stage longer than its follow-up window without action
  • Every opportunity has a dollar value (so reports are accurate)
  • Won and Lost deals are marked promptly -- with a reason for Lost
  • Nothing is "stuck" with no next step
The Value

Pipeline accuracy is directly tied to business revenue decisions. A VA who keeps it current is not just doing admin -- they are giving the owner reliable intel to act on.

Conversations -- The Unified Inbox

GHL's Conversations tab pulls every channel into one thread per contact: SMS, email, Facebook/Instagram DMs, web chat, and WhatsApp (when connected). Instead of jumping between apps, you see the entire history with a person in one place and reply from the same window.

Why this is powerful

A lead texts on Monday, emails on Wednesday, and DMs on Friday. In most businesses those are three disconnected messages. In GHL they're one continuous conversation, so you always have full context before you reply. No more "sorry, who is this again?"

Replying Like a Pro

  • Read the full thread first. Context prevents embarrassing mistakes.
  • Match the client's voice. This is where your Day 3 Project setup pays off -- Claude can draft replies in the client's exact tone.
  • Use snippets/templates for common replies, but personalise the first line.
  • Log the outcome -- update the tag or pipeline stage after the conversation moves forward.
Trust Scale Reminder (Day 1)

Messages to a client's leads are "Use Carefully." Someone real reads them. Always review AI-drafted replies for tone, accuracy, and the right name before you hit send. You represent the client with every message.

Day 6 · Session 6

SMS vs. Email vs. Call -- Which Channel to Use

Not all messages are created equal. The channel you choose affects whether leads respond, ignore, or feel pushed. Pick the right one and your reply rate goes up.

ChannelBest Used ForTypical Open RateVA Default Priority
📱 SMSQuick replies, reminders, time-sensitive follow-ups, re-engagement (short)~98% within 3 minFirst contact
✉ EmailLonger content, proposals, documents, nurture sequences, formal comms~20–25%Follow-up if SMS ignored 24–48 hrs
📞 CallHigh-value warm leads, deals stuck after 2+ messages, complaints, closingDepends on timingAfter 2+ touchpoints ignored
The VA default sequence

SMS first (fast, high open rate) → email if no reply in 24--48 hours → call if it's a warm or high-value deal stuck after two messages. Always check what the client's SOPs say -- some clients have specific channel rules that override the default.

Channel mismatch warning

Sending a long proposal over SMS, or texting a formal complaint response -- these feel off to the recipient and reflect poorly on the client. Channel choice is part of the brand experience. When in doubt, match what the client has done previously with that contact.
